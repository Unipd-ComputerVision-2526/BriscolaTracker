1    Introduction and Motivation
Briscola is a traditional Italian card game played with a 40-card regional deck. During a match, keeping track
of the points collected by each player is essential, but it can be difficult because point values are not ordered in
the same way as card ranks and because the score is accumulated over many rounds. The goal of this project is
to develop a computer vision system that analyses short videos of Briscola rounds and automatically extracts the
information needed to assign points and update the game score.
The proposed system focuses on the two-player version of the game. For each round, the input is a short video
clip showing the full action of the round, from the table state before the play to the moment when both players
have played and collected the cards.
The expected output is a structured description of the round: the two played cards, the card defining the trump
suit (briscola), the player associated with each card, the player who plays first, the winner of the round, and the
points assigned to that winner. By applying the system round by round, the final score of an entire game can be
reconstructed.


2    Task Definition
For each input video corresponding to one round, the system should:
1. detect and localize the visible cards on the table;
2. recognize the rank and suit of the two cards played in the round;
3. determine the briscola card used in the round, normally by recognizing the visible briscola card, but in the last
   videos of the game it will not be present;
4. associate each played card with the correct player: in the annotations, North and South denote the two fixed
   directions from which the cards come from (Above = North , Below = South) ;
5. determine the round winner;
6. compute the number of points collected in the round and update the cumulative score.
The main output for each round should follow the provided CSV format, where rank and suit are stored in separate
fields:
     Round, North Number, North Suit, South Number, South Suit,
     Briscola Number, Briscola Suit, Leader, Winner, Points.

The final output of a game should be provided in this form : the final totals of the accumulated points for each
player and the overall winner.


3    Brief Game Rules
For this project, only the rules needed to assign points are relevant. In each round, one player leads by playing the
first card and the other player answers with one card. The winner is determined as follows: If a card that has the
same suit as the briscola card is played, the highest ranking briscola card played wins. If the card that answers
the first one played is of different suti than the first one, the first card played wins. Else, if both cards have the
same suit, the highest ranking card wins.
The card ranking, from strongest to weakest, is:
                                      1 > 3 > 10 > 9 > 8 > 7 > 6 > 5 > 4 > 2

The point value assigned to each card is shown in Table 1.
The winner of the round receives as many points as the sum of the points of the two cards played in the round.

4    Dataset
                                      Dataset repository: Google Drive folder

The dataset contains recordings of Briscola rounds, reference card images, and ground-truth annotations. The
folder Briscola Trentine contains 40 scanned and labelled card images, one image per card, of the same Trentine
deck used in the videos. All the cards used in the videos are in this image dataset.
The dataset contains four complete games, each with 20 short videos, for a total of 80:

           Subset    Clips   Notes
           game1      20     controlled acquisition, clear pauses before card collection
           game2      20     controlled acquisition, similar setup with different lighting condition
           game3      20     more realistic play, with hands, occlusions, overlap, and less stable timing
           game4      20     controlled acquisition, different background

Ground truth for each game is provided in CSV files such as game1results.csv, game2results.csv,
game3results.csv, and game4results.csv. The CSV files contain one row per round with the exact fields
listed above: the round number, the number and suit of the card played by North, the number and suit of the card
played by South, the number and suit of the briscola card, the leading player, the round winner, and the points
assigned in that round. A corresponding text file, such as game1output.txt, stores the same round information
in a readable format and additionally reports the final game winner and the total points for both players. The
final result of the game is only reported in the .txt file.


5    Expected Challenges and Simplifications
The main visual challenges are small card details, rotated cards, similar suits, patterned tablecloth backgrounds,
partial card overlap, hands entering the scene, and the presence of irrelevant card stacks. The hardest subset is
expected to be the realistic game, where cards may be partially hidden and there may be no clean pause between
playing and collecting the cards.
The following simplifications are provided:
• the deck type is fixed to the Trentine cards provided as reference images;
• the camera viewpoint is fixed above the table for the whole game.


6    Performance Measurement
Performance is evaluated over one complete game by comparing the predicted outputs with the manually annotated
ground truth. Therefore, all 20 rounds of the game must be analyzed before computing the final metrics. The
evaluation is divided into card recognition, player identification, briscola recognition, and final result prediction.
• Card recognition accuracy: measures the proportion of played cards correctly recognized across the game.
  A card prediction is considered correct only if both rank and suit match the ground truth. Since two cards are
